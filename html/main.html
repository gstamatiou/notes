Chapter 1 Lorentzian spaces

Here we recall some basic facts on Lorentzian spaces. We will introduce Lorentzian manifolds of constant sectional curvature and we will see that, as in the Riemannian case, two Lorentzian manifolds of constant sectional curvature K𝐾K are locally isometric. Generally, we will focus on those with maximal isometry group, as they provide models of manifolds of constant sectional curvature: if M𝑀M is a Lorentzian manifold with constant sectional curvature K𝐾K and maximal isometry group, then any Lorentzian manifold with constant sectional curvature K𝐾K carries a natural (Isom​(M),M)Isom𝑀𝑀(\mathrm{Isom}(M),M)-atlas made of local isometries. Simply connected space forms have maximal isometry group, but in general there are manifolds with maximal isometry group which are not simply connected. In particular, we will focus on the case K=−1𝐾1K=-1 and in that case it will be convenient to use models which are not simply connected.

1.1 Basic definitions

Definition 1.1.1.
  1. (i)

    A Lorentzian metric on a manifold of dimension n+1𝑛1n+1 is a non-degenerate symmetric 2-tensor g𝑔g of signature (n,1)𝑛1(n,1).

  2. (ii)

    A Lorentzian manifold is a connected manifold M𝑀M equipped with a Lorentzian metric g𝑔g.

  3. (iii)

    In a Lorentzian manifold M𝑀M we say that a non-zero vector v∈T​M𝑣𝑇𝑀v\in TM is spacelike, lightlike, timelike if g​(v,v)𝑔𝑣𝑣g(v,v) is respectively positive, zero or negative. More generally, we say that a linear subspace V⊂Tx​M𝑉subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀V\subset T_{x}M is spacelike, lightlike, timelike if the restriction of gxsubscript𝑔𝑥g_{x} to V𝑉V is positive definite, degenerate or indefinite.

  4. (iv)

    A differentiable curve is spacelike, lightlike, timelike if its tangent vector is spacelike (resp. lightlike, timelike) at every point. It is causal if the tangent vector is either timelike or lightlike.

  5. (v)

    The set of lightlike vectors of Tx​Msubscript𝑇𝑥𝑀T_{x}M is also known as the light cone at x𝑥x.

Assuming dimM≥3dimension𝑀3\dim M\geq 3, the light cone disconnects Tx​Msubscript𝑇𝑥𝑀T_{x}M into three regions: two convex open cones formed by timelike vectors, one opposite to the other, and the region of spacelike vectors.

Definition 1.1.2.

Let M𝑀M be a Lorentzian manifold.

  1. (i)

    A continuous choice (in the sense of a continuous timelike vector field) of one of the two cones of time-like vectors for each point x∈M𝑥𝑀x\in M is called a time orientation of M𝑀M.

  2. (ii)

    If a time-orientation of M𝑀M exists, then M𝑀M is said to be time-orientable. Timelike vectors in the same component as the time-orientation are said future-directed, while the rest are said past-directed.

  3. (iii)

    Given a point x𝑥x in a time-oriented Lorentzian manifold M𝑀M, the future of x𝑥x is the set I+⁡(x)superscriptI𝑥\operatorname{\mathrm{I}^{+}}{(x)} of points which are connected to x𝑥x by a future-directed causal curve. 1.2 Maximal isometry groups and geodesic completeness

Constant curvature of manifolds allows us to extend isometries of tangent spaces to isometries of the whole manifold. As a result, two Lorentzian manifolds M𝑀M and N𝑁N of constant curvature K𝐾K are locally isometric, a fact which is well-known in the Riemannian setting. More precisely, the following holds:

Lemma 1.2.1.

Let M𝑀M and N𝑁N be Lorentzian manifolds of constant curvature K𝐾K.

  1. 1.

    Then every linear isometry L:Tx​M→Ty​N:𝐿→subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀subscript𝑇𝑦𝑁L:T_{x}M\to T_{y}N extends to an isometry f:U→V:𝑓→𝑈𝑉f:U\to V, where U𝑈U and V𝑉V are neighbourhoods of x𝑥x and y𝑦y respectively, and two extensions f:U→V:𝑓→𝑈𝑉f:U\to V and f:U′→V′:𝑓→superscript𝑈′superscript𝑉′f:U^{\prime}\to V^{\prime} of L𝐿L coincide on U∩U′𝑈superscript𝑈′U\cap U^{\prime}.

  2. 2.

    If M𝑀M is simply connected and N𝑁N is geodesically complete, then any isometry L:Tx​M→Ty​N:𝐿→subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀subscript𝑇𝑦𝑁L:T_{x}M\to T_{y}N extends to a unique local isometry f:M→N:𝑓→𝑀𝑁f:M\to N.

  3. 3.

    If M𝑀M and N𝑁N are both simply connected and geodesically complete, then any isometry L:Tx​M→Ty​N:𝐿→subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀subscript𝑇𝑦𝑁L:T_{x}M\to T_{y}N extends to a unique global isometry f:M→N:𝑓→𝑀𝑁f:M\to N.

Proof.

For the last statement, recall that a local isometry from a simply connected manifold to a uniquely geodesic manifold is a global isometry. ∎

Exactly as in the Riemannian case the proof is a simple consequence of the classical Cartan–Ambrose–Hicks Theorem. Note that this implies in particular that there is a unique simply connected geodesically complete Lorentzian manifold of constant curvature K𝐾K up to isometries. For instance for K=0𝐾0K=0 a model is the Minkowski space ℝn,1superscriptℝ𝑛1\mathbb{R}^{n,1}.

Another consequence of Lemma 1.2.1 is that, fixing a point x0∈Msubscript𝑥0𝑀x_{0}\in M, the set of isometries of M𝑀M, which we will denote by Isom​(M)Isom𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M), can be realized as a subset of ISO​(Tx0​M,T​M)ISOsubscript𝑇subscript𝑥0𝑀𝑇𝑀\mathrm{ISO}(T_{x_{0}}M,TM), namely the fiber bundle over M𝑀M whose fiber over x∈M𝑥𝑀x\in M is the space of linear isometries of Tx0​Msubscript𝑇subscript𝑥0𝑀T_{x_{0}}M into Tx​Msubscript𝑇𝑥𝑀T_{x}M.

It can be proved that Isom​(M)Isom𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M) has the structure of a Lie group with respect to composition so that the inclusion Isom​(M)↪ISO​(Tx0​M,T​M)↪Isom𝑀ISOsubscript𝑇subscript𝑥0𝑀𝑇𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M)\hookrightarrow\mathrm{ISO}(T_{x_{0}}M,TM) is a differentiable proper embedding. It follows that the maximal dimension of Isom​(M)Isom𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M) is dimO⁡(n,1)+n+1=(n+1)​(n+2)/2dimensionO𝑛1𝑛1𝑛1𝑛22\dim\operatorname{{\mathrm{O}}}(n,1)+n+1=(n+1)(n+2)/2.

Definition 1.2.1.

A Lorentzian manifold M𝑀M has maximal isometry group if the action of Isom​(M)Isom𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M) is transitive and, for every point x∈M𝑥𝑀x\in M, every linear isometry L:Tx​M→Tx​M:𝐿→subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀subscript𝑇𝑥𝑀L:T_{x}M\to T_{x}M extends to an isometry of M𝑀M.

Equivalently M𝑀M has maximal isometry group if the above inclusion of Isom​(M)Isom𝑀\mathrm{Isom}(M) into the bundle ISO​(Tx0​M,T​M)ISOsubscript𝑇subscript𝑥0𝑀𝑇𝑀\mathrm{ISO}(T_{x_{0}}M,TM) is a bijection. Hence, if M𝑀M has maximal isometry group, then the dimension of the isometry group is maximal.

From Lemma 1.2.1, every simply connected Lorentzian manifold M𝑀M has maximal isometry group if it has constant sectional curvature and is geodesically complete. The converse holds even without the simply connectedness assumption:

Lemma 1.2.2.

  1. Let M𝑀M be a Lorentzian manifold.

  2. (i)

    If M𝑀M has a maximal isometry group then M𝑀M has constant sectional curvature and is geodesically complete.

  3. (ii)

    If M𝑀M is simply connected, then M𝑀M has maximal isometry group if and only if M𝑀M has constant sectional curvature and is geodesically complete.
